127 Years of History

CLUB
HISTORY

1897

Club Founded

Juventus Football Club founded on November 1, 1897 by a group of students in Turin. The club originally wore pink shirts before adopting the iconic black and white stripes in 1903.

1923

Agnelli Family Takes Over

Edoardo Agnelli, son of Fiat founder Giovanni Agnelli, becomes president. The Agnelli family's stewardship begins a new era of professionalism and ambition for the club.

1930s

First Golden Era — Five Consecutive Titles

Juventus win five consecutive Serie A titles from 1930 to 1935, establishing themselves as Italy's dominant force with legends like Giampiero Combi and Felice Borel.

1985

European Champions

Juventus win the European Cup (now Champions League) defeating Liverpool 1-0 in the Heysel Stadium final, with Michel Platini scoring the winning penalty.

1996

Champions League Glory Again

A second European Cup triumph, defeating Ajax on penalties in Rome. A squad featuring Del Piero, Vialli, Zidane and Conte cements Juventus as a true continental superpower.

2006

Calciopoli and Relegation

Juventus are relegated to Serie B following the Calciopoli scandal — a dark chapter. The club bounce straight back to Serie A as champions, showing true resilience.

2012

The Nine-Peat Begins

Under Antonio Conte, Juventus begin an unprecedented run of nine consecutive Serie A titles — the longest winning streak in the history of any top European league.

2023

New Era Under Allegri

Juventus continue their rebuild under Massimiliano Allegri, with a young squad led by Vlahović and Chiesa targeting a return to Champions League glory.
